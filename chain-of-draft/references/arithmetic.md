# Arithmetic / Math — CoD Few-Shot Examples

Use these examples for word problems, multi-step calculations, percentages, and general arithmetic reasoning.

## Few-shot set (pick 3-4)

### Example 1: Simple subtraction
Q: Jason had 20 lollipops. He gave Denny some lollipops. Now Jason has 12 lollipops. How many lollipops did Jason give to Denny?
A: 20 - 12 = 8. #### 8

### Example 2: Multi-step addition
Q: Shawn has five toys. For Christmas, he got two toys each from his mom and dad. How many toys does he have now?
A: 2+2=4 new; 5+4=9. #### 9

### Example 3: Subtraction word problem
Q: There are 15 trees in the grove. Grove workers will plant trees in the grove today. After they are done, there will be 21 trees. How many trees did the grove workers plant today?
A: 21 - 15 = 6. #### 6

### Example 4: Multi-step with multiplication
Q: If there are 3 cars in the parking lot and 2 more cars arrive, each with 4 passengers, how many people are in the cars total in the parking lot?
A: 3 cars empty; 2×4=8 people. #### 8

### Example 5: Percentage / ratio
Q: A store had 200 apples. They sold 40% on Monday and half of the remainder on Tuesday. How many are left?
A: 200×0.4=80 sold Mon; 120 left; 120/2=60 Tue. #### 60

### Example 6: Multi-step with fractions
Q: Olivia has $23. She bought five bagels for $3 each. How much money does she have left?
A: 5×3=15 spent; 23-15=8. #### 8

## Usage notes

- For GSM8K-style problems, 3 examples are usually sufficient
- Order examples from simple to slightly complex
- Each draft step should be a pure calculation: operands, operator, result
- Avoid variable names or story context in the draft
- Use semicolons to separate steps when multiple operations are needed
